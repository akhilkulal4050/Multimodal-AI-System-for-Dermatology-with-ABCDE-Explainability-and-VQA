Turn 4 — Treatment

Patient asks what comes next
What treatment would be needed if this is confirmed as melanoma?
If confirmed as melanoma following biopsy, the standard treatment pathway is:

1. Wide Local Excision (WLE): Surgical removal of the lesion with safety margins of 1–2 cm depending on Breslow depth. This is the primary curative treatment.

2. Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB): For lesions thicker than 0.8mm (T1b+), the nearest lymph nodes are sampled to check for microscopic spread.

3. Adjuvant therapy for advanced stages: If the melanoma has spread to lymph nodes or distant sites, immunotherapy (pembrolizumab or nivolumab — anti-PD-1 antibodies) or targeted therapy (dabrafenib + trametinib for BRAF V600E mutation) may be used.

Given the URGENT risk score we discussed, the dermatologist will likely proceed to excisional biopsy at the first appointment to confirm the diagnosis before planning further treatment.
