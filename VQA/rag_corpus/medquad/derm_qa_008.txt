Q: What color variations indicate malignancy in skin lesions?
A: Presence of 5 or 6 dermoscopic colors is strongly associated with melanoma. The six evaluated colors are: light brown, dark brown, black (superficial or deep melanin), red (vascularity or inflammation), white (regression or fibrosis), and blue-grey (melanin in dermis). Benign lesions typically show only 1-2 colors.
