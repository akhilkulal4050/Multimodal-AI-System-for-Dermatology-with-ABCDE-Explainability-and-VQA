Q: What is actinic keratosis?
A: Actinic keratosis (AK) is a common premalignant epithelial lesion from cumulative UV exposure. Presents as rough, scaly patches on sun-damaged skin. Histologically shows atypical keratinocyte proliferation in the lower epidermis. Annual risk of individual AK progressing to invasive SCC is 0.1-0.5%. Treatment: cryotherapy, topical 5-fluorouracil, imiquimod, ingenol mebutate, photodynamic therapy, diclofenac gel.
