Q: When should a mole be removed?
A: Mole removal is indicated when ABCDE criteria are present, the lesion has changed rapidly, bleeds or ulcerates spontaneously, dermoscopy reveals atypical features (irregular network, regression, atypical vessels), or clinician is uncertain. Any lesion suspicious for melanoma should be excised with 1-2mm margins for histopathological diagnosis rather than observed.
